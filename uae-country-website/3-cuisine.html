The Cuisine

Traditional Cuisine

Like everywhere, Emirati food reflected the region’s culture, climate, and resources. After discovering oil and the influx of expats, their cuisine underwent many changes.

Famous Dishes in UAE

Khuzi

Khuzi is the national dish of the UAE, made from roasted mutton that is set atop a bed of fragrant rice and vegetables and topped with roasted nuts and raisins.

Shawarma

Shawarma is not a specific dish but instead a method of meat preparation. It is shaved off in small chunks and typically stuffed into pockets of pita bread.

Machboos

Machboos is a rice dish seasoned with aromatic spices and served with grilled meats, often mutton, lamb, or chicken. This dish is very similar to Indian Biryani.

Balaleet

Balaleet is a savory and sweet dish made by mixing noodles with sugar, cardamom, rose water, and saffron. The dish is topped with a fried egg omelet.

Samboosa

A fried triangle pastry filled with spiced meat, vegetables, cheese, and nuts. The Arabic take on the Indian samosa, typically thinner.

Knafeh

A sweet, cheesy, cake-like dessert dish made from kataifi dough that is soaked in syrup. This can be completed with an assortment of toppings; nuts, raisins, or clotted cream.

Chebab

A great choice for breakfast, Chebab is a yeasted pancake flavored with cardamom and saffron and topped with butter, honey, date syrup, or jam.

Luqaimat

luqaimat are deep-fried dumplings that are dipped or drizzled in sweeteners; honey, sugar syrup, or date syrup. A treat often enjoyed at cafes alongside classic arab coffee.
